Zhou Zhi (1978–2022) | Memorial Archive
Basic information
Place of origin: Shanghai
Identity: photographer; researcher of visual systems; former senior structural engineer.
Death: born in Shanghai in 1978; once worked at a national architectural design institute; in April 2022, in Shanghai (during lockdown), he died of chronic heart failure and respiratory complications.
Education: 1996–2000, Department of Architectural Engineering, Tongji University.
2005–2007: invited short-term visiting research at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar (Germany) on “industrial vision and automation of perception”.

II. Artist quotes
Website loading screen
On making: “In this era, ‘inspiration’ is the enemy of efficiency. I never create; I only execute.”
On humans: “The artist is a biological error in the production chain. My ultimate goal is to eliminate the ‘human smell’ from photography and return images to their truth as optical data.”
On construction sites (field research): “A construction site is a miniature of contemporary biopolitics. There are no individuals there—only labour-power that has been gridded.”
On death: “When the last work order is issued, when measurement error returns to zero, the artist completes the process of being deregistered from the system.”

III. Representative photographic series
1. Axonometric Discipline (2015–2018)


Description: shot with an ultra-high-resolution digital back, focusing on fragments of construction sites. The images cancel all vanishing points, presenting an absolutely rational parallel structure.
Academic phrasing: “By violently correcting perspective, Zhou Zhi strips the viewer of the privilege of being a ‘subject’, reducing the architectural landscape to a pure matrix of power.”

Axonometric Discipline 1 — Pre-arrangement: materials and components are organised in advance as standardised units, spread through space in parallel logic.

Axonometric Discipline 2 — Silent standby: containers, rebar, and modular components are absorbed into the same managerial plane.


Axonometric Discipline 3 — Operational layer: machinery and temporary structures appear side by side without perspective, forming a pure distribution of function.

Deliverables: 0.00mm (2019–2021)


Description: photographs abandoned tie wires, dried concrete fragments, and piling marks left behind on sites. Each image is accompanied by a precise “Material Inspection Report”.
Academic phrasing: “A forensic collection of labour traces. Through the lens, Zhou Zhi records the last remnants of ‘nameless labour’ before the system swallows them.”

Silent Sampling (2022)


Description: his last unfinished body of work. Shot during his quarantine in Shanghai in 2022. The frames contain only sliced window shadows and extremely fine airborne dust.
Academic phrasing: “When the external production line stalls, Zhou Zhi turns his ‘measuring instrument’ toward life itself. This series foreshadows the systemic collapse of his individual body.”

Special details of the memorial website
Website name: ZZ Archive: The Zhou Zhi Estate
Introduction: This site is the official digital stub for visual-systems researcher Zhou Zhi (1978–2022). During his lifetime, Zhou devoted himself to bringing engineering metrology into image production, aiming to strip photography of its lyrical tradition. As his final “system deliverable”, this archive collects practices around biopolitics, industrial modules, and spatial discipline. His death marks the final closure of the “affectless sampling” experiment— the creative subject has been formally deregistered; only the system remains present.

Footer copyright: “This website is jointly operated by the Zhou Zhi Estate Trust and the Society for System Photography Studies. Certain technical data is protected by law.”
Work-ID system: every photo on the site has a corresponding code, e.g., ZZ-2019-CONC-082 (Zhou Zhi – 2019 – Concrete series – No. 082).
Technical parameters disclosure: beside each image are the GPS coordinates, wind speed, shutter time precise to 1/10,000 of a second, and the CRC checksum of the post-processing algorithm.
System UI & interaction elements
Status markers:
Seal / warning: images are overlaid with a translucent watermark such as “Archive Sealed” or “Copyright belongs to the Estate Trust”.
Progress bar: perpetually stuck at 99.9%, or a looping system-error dialogue box.
